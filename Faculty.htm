THERESA LUNGARO-MIFSUD trained in classical ballet, Jazz and contemporary dance at Tanya Bayona Academy. From a young age she was aware of the importance of furthering her studies and attended several courses at The Academia Nazionale di Danza in Rome, The Conservetoire de Danse et Musique in Paris as well as private courses in London.

At the age of 19 she was offered a teaching post with Tanya Bayona where she was able to keep up her dance training as well as develop her teaching skills. She gained her fellowship in 1991 and in 1992 following a year teaching and dancing in Newcastle-Upon-Tyne; she passed her Enrico Cecchetti Diploma. Upon her return to Malta, Theresa took up the post of Assistant director with Tanya Bayona Princess Poutiatine Academy of Ballet and she performed regularly as prinicipal dancer with the school company in both local and international festivals.

In 1999, she founded The Dance Workshop; here she is responsible for both the academic and artistic sides of the school. Since last October, she has been running the Foundation and Certificate of Dance Education Courses (teacher training) for the ISTD. Every year she co-ordinates the school’s dance production which has been held for the last 13 years at St. Agatha’s Auditorium, creating an adapted ballet for the lower and middle school as well as choreographing short pieces for the senior ballet students.

In 2006, Theresa was awarded a scholarship to attend the international summer school organised by the Cecchetti Council America held in Michigan. This was the second scholarship given to Ms Lungaro-Mifsud by the Cecchetti Society; the first was to attend a summer course in the UK in 2000. Theresa is the local Exam Organiser (Malta) for the ISTD and she is very proud to be the first Maltese examiner for ISTD. Theresa is a regular guest teacher at the Cecchetti International Summer School, held annually in the UK. She has also been invited to teach in Italy, Norway and Cyprus.

In 2012, Theresa was awarded a scholarship by Mr. Raymond Lukens, to attend Level 1 of the American Ballet Theatre curriculum. She followed this course in August 2013, in Italy, where she was awarded yet another scholarship to attend Level 2 of the same course. Following these courses, Theresa is now an ABT® Certified Teacher, who has successfully completed the ABT® Teacher training Intensive in Pre-Primary through Level 5 of the ABT® National Training Curriculum.

PAMELA KERR started her ballet training at the Tanya Bayona Academy. By the age of ten she also started Flamenco under the direction of Rosanna Maya. In 1998, after spending six months studying at the London Studio Centre, Pamela returned to Malta and resumed her dance training locally under the direction of Theresa Lungaro-Mifsud at The Dance Workshop. She also started exploring contemporary dance under the guidance of Francesca Abela Tranter. She participated in various festivals and performances, both locally and abroad. She has also been asked to guest dance with various companies. In 2004, Pamela obtained her Enrico Cecchetti Diploma becoming the first Maltese candidate to pass as a dancer. She was awarded her Fellowship teaching qualification in August 2009. Pamela teaches classical ballet at The Dance Workshop and is also a practising assistant psychologist.

FRANCESCA BARTOLO started ballet lessons at the Tanya Bayona Academy. She then joined The Dance Workshop, when it was founded in 1999, attending both classical ballet and Flamenco classes. While at The Dance Workshop, Francesca received the ‘Junior Associate Award’ in 2002 as well as the ‘Spanish Dance Award’ in 2007. In 2008 she attended the Cecchetti Summer School in Chichester and two years later participated in the International Ballet Master Classes in Prague alongside vocational students from various international dance schools. Francesca was awarded her Diploma in Dance Education (DDE) in 2012 and as part of her training towards obtaining her Licentiate qualification; she has recently attended the Kate Simmons Dance International Summer School. She read for a degree in B.Ed Primary (Hons) and has been working as a primary teacher in an independent school for the past three years.


MARIA LOUISE GINGELL teaches classical ballet. She started her ballet training at Tanya Bayona Princess Poutiatine Academy of Ballet. She pursued thorough training in the various disciplines including Classical Ballet, Spanish and Contemporary. She was awarded various scholarships in dance in Malta and abroad, namely at Bush Davis College and the London Studio Centre. In 2000, she passed her Associate Examination of the ISTD with honours and was invited to join the teaching faculty at The Dance Workshop. She was awarded her Associate Diploma in 2004 and has completed her Certificate in Dance Education in 2007.

DOUGLAS COMLEY completed his studies at Middlesex University with a BA Honours First Degree in Performance Arts. He was also Creative Arts Wales Award winner in 2010. He started his professional career with Nexus Dance Company. From then on, he worked as a performer internationally with companies Attik Dance, Daghdha Dance Company, Vincent Dance Theatre, Bodies in Urban Spaces, Carlson Dance. He has been assistant choreographer on many projects under the community dance organisation T.A.N Dance. These have included working and choreographing with Ndere Dance Company in Uganda and The Creators in South Africa which won a Youth in Excellence Award 1997. From 1996 Douglas has been resident Dance Course Lecturer on BTEC and National Diploma in Performing Arts, AS Dance at Swansea College and Gower College of Further Education. Douglas is senior tutor for TAN Dance Community projects. He has worked for various groups including Education for Adulthood - introducing dance to disadvantaged youth, The Young Offenders - Northlands Art Centre, Cheltenham with young offenders and their probation officers. He is a qualified Soundbeam Music Technician introducing music to special needs groups of all ages and abilities. Douglas has commissioned to work with Danish Youth Company (Juniorkompagniet), Contact Dance Company (Malta) and led workshops in Male Dance at School Of Modern Dance in Copenhagen and Deutcher Sportlehrerverband, Germany 2006 to 2015. As Director/Choreographer of Dynion Male Dance Company he has created 12 productions for the community company and toured them worldwide. Douglas is also currently artistic director of the County Youth Dance Company in Wales


KOSTAS PAPAMATTHAIAKIS is a freelance dancer and dance teacher giving classes in contemporary dance and traditional Greek folk dance. His approach in teaching draws influences from Limon Technique combined with principles from Released-based Technique and Floor Work. Kostas graduated with a BA (Hons.) from Greek National Opera Dance School in Athens. During his studies he worked with independent choreographers and dance companies such as Aenaon Dance Theatre – Daniel Lommel, the Greek National Opera and the Hellenic Dance Company. Through scholarships Kostas had the opportunity not only to travel and be part of prestigious summer programmes such as Jacob’s Pillow in Massachusetts and Joffrey Ballet School in New York but also to further his studies in dance. In 2011 he moved to the UK to obtain his Postgraduate Diploma from London Contemporary Dance School. Soon after, Kostas joined Candoco Dance Company with whom he performed at the Closing Ceremony of the Paralympic Games in London and toured internationally, in more than 20 countries, performing pieces by Rachid Ouramadane, Emanuel Gat, Trisha Brown and two new creations by Javier de Frutos and Thomas Hauert. Later, in 2014 he was a member of Malta’s National Dance Company – ZfinMalta, performing locally as well as in Palestine and Israel, pieces by Mavin Khoo, Jose Agudo and Francesca Tranter.


JADE FARRUGIA started Ballet lessons at the age of 3 at Pauleen's Dance and Fitness Studio and joined The Dance Workshop in 2005, at the age of 11. As well as dancing ballet, in 1999 she started Jazz and Lyrical dance under the direction of Natasha Micallef and later on, also started contemporary under the direction of Francesca Abela-Tranter (2009). Jade has attended many international workshops throughout her training such as International Ballet Master Classes at the Prague School of Ballet; the Cecchetti Summer School in Chichester; Summer Intensive at Ateneo Danza in Forli; Mediterraneo Dance Festival in Reggio Calabria, and Spazio Libero Malta. She has been awarded prestigious scholarships to attend intensive courses such as World Dance Movement in Bari, Mediterraneo Dance Festival in Italy and Summer Intensive at The Dance Workshop. Jade has also received the Margot Fonteyn Award from The Dance Workshop. In October 2007, Jade obtained her Intermediate qualification in Modern Theatre and in December 2010, she obtained her Advanced 2 Cecchetti Ballet qualification. She is currently working towards her Diploma in Dance Instruction (DDI) and reading towards a Degree in Bachelor of Engineering and Architecture (Honours).


CLAIRE CASSOLA is responsible for the Jazz faculty at The Dance Workshop. She started her training at the age of 16 with Felix Busuttil and later joined the academy of Tanya Bayona. In 1989 Claire won first Prize in an International Contemporary Festival held in Sicily with choreography by Francesca Abela and in 1992 she was awarded the Brigit Espinosa – London Studio Centre Scholarship where she had the opportunity to work with international choreographers and teachers. Claire has established herself locally as a principal dancer taking on several leading roles during performances with Tanya Bayona Dance Theatre, YADA and The Dance Workshop. She is a qualified Modern Dance Teacher and is currently working with The Dance Workshop, The College of Jazz and St. Michael's Foundation. She was awarded the Associate Diploma in 2004 and her Licentiate in October 2007 and her CDE in 2010.

DIANE PORTELLI started her training at the age 3 at Tanya Bayona Academy of Ballet. In 1999, she furthered her training at The Dance Workshop, where she is currently a teacher and choreographer. She was awarded the Luisello award for Spanish Dance in 1996 and the Duke of Ellington award for Modern Dance in 1998. In 2000, she participated in the World Championships for Performing Arts in Hollywood, together with two other dancers, where they won two gold medals under the direction of Janet Vella. In 2004, she furthered her dance training in Pavia for a year. Last year she was also selected for an international workshop with Random Dance Company. She has performed in various international festivals including Portugal, Hong Kong, Tunisia and Brussels amongst others. Diane obtained a Bachelor’s Degree in Commerce in 2003 and is currently working towards her DDE qualifications in modern theatre. She is currently working with Moveo Dance Company as a performer.

BETTINA VON BROCKDORFF has taken over the Flamenco dance faculty at The Dance Workshop as from this year. She is a flamenco and danza estilizada teacher. She is also the director of the flamenco company Inspiracion Flamenca. Bettina began her dance training in ballet and furthered with Spanish Dance in Flamenco. She was awarded the Malta Arts Scholarship and studied Spanish dance professionally in Jerez de la Frontera in Spain. She currently teaches freelance and directs her company.

SUE VASSALLO started her Jazz training at Brigitte Gauci Borda School of Dance at the age of eight, and now has ten years teaching experience. She has taught dance for musical theatre performances and currently teaches Jazz and Hip Hop. Sue has performed and choreographed for many shows including, The Goldwell Hair Awards, Junior Eurovision, Dance Power Four, Fashion Awards, The Beer Festival, The Bay Music Awards, the ‘Nescafe Dolce Gusto’ advert and the ‘Kinnie’ advert. Sue was also asked to choreograph and perform for American Hip Hop icons JA RULE and FLO RIDA when they performed in Malta.

Sue’s choreographies have always done well in dance competitions, usually placing in the top three. Internationally, she presented a choreography at the ‘Barcelona Costa Brava International Dance Festival’ (Spain), attended intensive courses ‘Broadway a Tropea’ (Italy), ‘World Dance Movement’ (Italy) where she received a Hip Hop award and preformed one of her choreographies. Sue also accompanied 30 students to Pineapple Studios (UK). Most recently, she attended a two week intensive course in Los Angeles at the infamous commercial studios, Millennium Dance Studios and Debbie Renalds Dance Studios. She was granted this scholarship, along with her student, from world famous choreographer Shane Sparks.

Dance training includes courses with teachers from America, England, Italy, Germany, France, Holland, Brazil and Malta. Sue is currently teaches Brigitte Gauci Borda School of Ballet, The Dance Workshop and Incite Motion. She is currently working towards obtaining her DDE in Modern Dance.
